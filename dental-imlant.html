What is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant is a strong and small titanium screw which is placed into prepared sites in the jawbone, replacing your missing tooth root. After a short healing period, the bone grows onto the surface of the implant (A process known as Osseo-integration) so it can anchor a replacement tooth(crown) securely to the implant.

What Are the Advantages of Dental Implants?

Dental implants seamlessly emulate the look, feel, and functionality of natural teeth. Unlike traditional bridges that often require sacrificing healthy tooth structure from adjacent teeth, dental implants eliminate the need to grind down neighboring teeth, preserving their integrity.

With dental implants, you can enjoy eating and speaking comfortably and confidently. Say goodbye to the need to hide gaps in your smile with your hand or worry about dentures slipping or loosening when laughing. Embrace the joy of smiling and laughing during good times with unwavering confidence.

With a proven track record spanning over 40 years, dental implants have demonstrated remarkable success in millions of individuals worldwide as a preferred solution for missing teeth. High success rates, reaching up to 95% with optimal oral and general health, have been consistently reported in thirty-year studies.

What's Involved in Implant Dentistry?

Comprehensive Examination :
In your initial consultation, provided at no cost, the dentist conducts a thorough dental examination, captures photographs, takes x-rays of your dentition, and discusses treatment options. Before your second visit, a bone scan of the relevant jaw bone is recommended.

Dental Implant Placement :
The dental implant is carefully inserted into the jawbone, a procedure conducted under local anesthesia at our surgery. Postoperative care instructions are provided, and healing time is contingent on factors such as bone quantity, quality, and the specific clinical situation.

Abutment Attachment :
After the implant achieves Osseo-integration, it is uncovered, and an abutment is affixed to it.

Crown Installation :
The final step involves attaching your new artificial tooth (crown) to the dental implant.

Addressing the Issue:

For those experiencing tooth loss due to periodontal disease, trauma, injury, or any other cause, there is a proven solution – dental implants. This option is suitable for individuals with good overall oral and systemic health.

Dental implants stand out as one of the most reliable and conservative methods for replacing missing teeth, ensuring the best long-term success. These implants serve as artificial tooth roots, strategically placed into the jawbone to provide support for teeth or crowns.

The remarkable feature of dental implants lies in their ability to replicate the look, feel, and function of natural teeth. When appropriately cared for, they can endure a lifetime. Various forms of dental implants are available, with "root form implants" being the most common. If you're seeking a lasting solution to tooth loss, dental implants offer a comprehensive and effective approach.

Exploring Your Alternatives:

Consider the option of root form implants, which closely resemble natural teeth by mimicking tooth roots.

The procedure involves placing the implants beneath the skin and into the bone during the initial surgical appointment, conducted in the dental practice under local anesthesia, with or without oral sedation. Following this, a healing period of two (2) to six (6) months allows the dental implants to integrate seamlessly with the bone.

At a subsequent brief surgical appointment, the implants are exposed before a post is inserted. This post serves as the extension to which a crown is attached. The entire process typically spans four (4) to seven (7) months.

Considering the Consequences of Inaction:

If no action is taken, the consequences of tooth loss can be significant. However, dental implants offer a proactive solution to this dilemma. These implants employ small anchors crafted from biocompatible titanium metal, which are strategically placed into the jawbone. An advantage of titanium is its resistance to decay, eliminating the need for root canal therapy.

As the bone gradually grows around these anchors in the first few months, they become securely embedded in the jaw. Subsequently, abutment posts are introduced into the anchors, facilitating the permanent attachment of meticulously crafted replacement teeth. This transformative process results in the restoration of natural-looking and feeling teeth.

Is the investment in dental implants worthwhile?
Opting for dental implants is a highly beneficial decision if you desire a prosthetic tooth that not only functions seamlessly but also mimics the natural appeal of your original tooth. Comprising metallic screws that emulate the role of a natural tooth's root, dental implants provide a sturdy foundation for the attached dental crown.

Beyond their cosmetic and functional advantages, dental implants contribute to the support and preservation of your jawbone, mitigating the risks associated with local jawbone loss. Choosing a dental implant to replace a missing tooth not only promotes overall oral health but also prevents the potential shifting of neighboring teeth. With an exceptional success rate, dental implants offer enduring results, making them a reliable and lasting solution.
What is the lifespan of dental implants?
A significant benefit of dental implants is their remarkable longevity. Anticipate your implant offering around 20 years of reliable service with minimal maintenance. Through diligent oral hygiene practices, you can extend the durability of your dental implant to surpass 30 years.

Crucially, the prosthetic demands no specialized care and maintenance, providing consistent results and boasting minimal chances of implant failure.
Can dental implants develop cavities?
Dental implants are resistant to cavities because they are constructed from titanium, a material that oral bacteria cannot degrade. Additionally, many dental crowns used in conjunction with implants are crafted from materials such as porcelain or porcelain-metal blends, which are highly resilient to acid attacks.

Despite their resilience, maintaining excellent oral hygiene is crucial for the long-term success of dental implants. Accumulation of plaque and tartar around the implants can contribute to local jawbone loss, potentially leading to implant failure.
Is it feasible to substitute all teeth with implants?
For those seeking a comprehensive dental renewal, the All-On-4 dental restoration stands out as the ideal choice for achieving a fully restored smile. As the name implies, this procedure involves the placement of merely four implants to secure a complete bridge along your jaw, ensuring the attainment of an impeccable smile.

Renowned for its popularity and cost-effectiveness, the All-On-4 procedure requires covering the expense of only four implants. Furthermore, the procedure's simplicity and minimal associated discomfort promise a swift recovery.
Is it more advisable to undergo a root canal or opt for an implant?
The choice between a root canal and an implant depends on the specific condition of your tooth. In instances where your tooth exhibits significant damage or cavities that surpass the feasibility of a root canal, your dentist might recommend a dental implant. However, if a root canal procedure can effectively salvage your tooth, your dentist will likely proceed with that option, allowing you to preserve your natural tooth.

It's crucial to note that retaining your natural tooth is generally considered the optimal choice whenever possible. To determine the most suitable approach for your situation, consult with your dentist to explore whether a root canal procedure can successfully preserve your tooth.
