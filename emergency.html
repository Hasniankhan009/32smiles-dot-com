Emergency Dental

Dental injuries often arise as the result of an accident or fall. But it is not the only cause of dental issues. Any dental problem that requires immediate medical attention to soothe the pain, fix the injury or stop the bleeding is considered a dental emergency. It is essential that with some emergencies, seeking professional medical help within 30 minutes of injury is essential to save the tooth or teeth. We have a team of experienced professionals offering emergency dental treatment should you ever have the misfortune of needing it. Our expert urgent dentists are ready to provide immediate care, even after hours, ensuring your dental emergencies are handled effectively.

Lost crown

If you’ve lost a crown and still have it, rinse the inside and contact your dentist as soon as possible. If it is still suitable, your dentist can reattach the crown for you, otherwise they will advise you of an alternative treatment. Do not try to re-attach it yourself.

Experience expert solutions for a lost dental crown, ensuring your smile’s restoration and care.

Lost Filling

If you have lost a filling, it is best to contact your dentist straight away, regardless of whether it is hurting or not. Prompt treatment of a lost filling will can prevent the need to have more complex and costly treatment down the track.
If you don’t need our emergency service and want any other services for your teeth like fix braces, wisdom teeth removal or any other, you can just give us a call. Visit us for quick and professional eplacement of your lost filling.

Fractured And Chipped Tooth

Depending on the seriousness of the damage, a dentist may be able to assist with fractured teeth if treatment is sought immediately. If there is severe pain the dentist may perform a root canal for example. However, in the case of a significant fracture, the tooth might be too damaged and need extraction. Trust Belleville Dental to restore your smile with expert care for fractured and chipped teeth.
When unexpected dental issues arise, our after-hours dentists are here to deliver the highest quality of emergency dental care, putting your comfort and health first.

Toothaches

Tooth pain has a wide variety of causes, from bruxism (teeth grinding) to cavities, gum disease, an exposed root, or even an ear or sinus infection. That’s why it’s important to consult both your doctor and your dentist if you’re having significant, consistent tooth pain. In the meantime, most over-the-counter pain medication can reduce pain and inflammation. A cold compress on the outside of your cheek may also help numb pain around the jaws. Facial pain around the teeth, jaw, mouth, gums, and wounds inside the mouth are all considered dental emergencies.
Other symptoms of dental emergencies include significant bleeding in the mouth, broken teeth, experiencing facial trauma or severe pain in jaws or swelling of mouth or around gums, and loose/hanging teeth. Relieve toothaches effectively with compassionate dental care.

Knocked Out Tooth

If you have the tooth, hold it by the crown and gently rinse any dirt away from the root. As soon as possible after the tooth has fallen out, we recommend you put the tooth in a small cup of milk, or if possible, saline – this will encourage cell growth and prevent the tooth from drying out. Call emergency dentists straight away – the sooner you can reinsert the tooth, the better chance you have at saving it.

A knocked out tooth can be reinserted back by the dentist if the patient follows emergency procedures and resorts to professional dentist assistance in a timely manner. These include either holding the tooth by the crown or inserting it back into the mouth where it fell out. If it is inserted back into the mouth, try to keep it in place and level with the other teeth by biting on gauze. For urgent solutions to a knocked-out tooth, rely on us for expert dental care.

Loose Tooth

A loose tooth should not be ignored. It can disturb the alignment of its adjacent teeth and can cause pain. Immediate dental assistance should be sought in this case where the dentist can assess and determine what needs to be done. Safeguard your oral health with professional assistance for a loose tooth at Belleville Dental.
Don’t let dental emergencies disrupt your life – trust the experienced professionals to provide top-notch emergency dental services when you need them the most.

Emergency Dentist FaQs

What is meant by dental emergency?
Any new and sudden development that deteriorates your oral health is a dental emergency. If you are unsure about a dental emergency, the following list will tell you whether you should get prioritized dental care:

⦁ Severe dental pain
⦁ Bleeding from the mouth
⦁ A loose or wobbly tooth
⦁ Knocked-out tooth
⦁ Oral infection
⦁ Dislodged dental prosthetics
You should get immediate dental care to address the pain and all oral health concerns as soon as you struggle with any of the above scenarios.
What is the most common dental emergency?
Tooth infection and pain are the most common dental emergencies that demand immediate dental assistance. If left untreated, a tooth infection can become a serious oral health issue and lead to nerve damage.
Additionally, getting emergency dental care as soon as you notice tooth infection or pain can minimise the cost of dental restoration.
What helps unbearable tooth pain?
You can do several at-home remedies to get some comfort before visiting the emergency dentist. Try gargling lukewarm saltwater to remove bacteria and reduce the swelling. Also, you can place an ice pack against your cheek to get some comfort and numb the pain. Taking over-the-counter pain killers like aspirin can relieve the pain and help you relax. Don’t forget that these home remedies only give you temporary relief, and you should get emergency dental care to address the oral health issue.
In case of a dental emergency at night, what do I need to do?
It is important for you to identify whether you are in a critical situation that requires immediate dental care. Certain dental emergencies require prompt care, whereas others may not be as urgent. If you are struggling with any of the following dental conditions, you should reach out to your dentist right away for quick care and assistance:

⦁ Injured jaw
⦁ Painful toothache that doesn’t subside with the at-home remedies or painkillers
⦁ A knocked-out or dislodged tooth
⦁ Extreme tooth sensitivity
⦁ Painful facial swelling
⦁ Bleeding gums that don’t stop
What is considered an emergency dental procedure??
Emergency dental procedures are any dental procedures that are aimed to address pain, bleeding, or infection. Several dental health issues demand emergency dental care to address bleeding or relieve pain, including:

⦁ Broken or knocked-out tooth
⦁ Toothache
⦁ Infection on gums or jaws
⦁ Tooth sensitivity
⦁ Jaw and gum injury
⦁ Facial swelling
⦁ Accidents that lead to oral injury
⦁ Dislodged or loose dental prosthetics, braces, or dentures.

If you struggle with any of the above, reach out and visit us for quick treatment services. You will get prioritized dental care to address the issue and relieve the pain.
