Wisdom Teeth Removal

Why Should I Consider Wisdom Teeth Removal?

When there isn't sufficient space in your mouth for the full eruption of your third molars, various issues may arise. The extraction of impacted wisdom teeth becomes necessary to prevent potential damage to adjacent teeth. Wisdom teeth problems typically arise between the ages of 17-25, with an increased likelihood of issues after the age of 30.

In the circumstances outlined below, wisdom teeth removal is generally recommended at our clinic. However, it is always advisable to consult with a dentist to assess your eligibility for specific treatments and procedures.

Infection

The common clinical issue we encounter is pericoronitis, which is a localized gum infection. When there is insufficient space for complete eruption, the gum tissue around the wisdom tooth may become irritated and infected, leading to recurring pain, swelling, and difficulties with chewing and/or swallowing.

Formation of Cysts

In connection with an impacted wisdom tooth, non-infectious conditions can also emerge. Cysts, which are fluid-filled sacs within the jaw bone, can develop due to impacted teeth. These cysts slowly expand, leading to the destruction of adjacent jaw bone and teeth. While cysts may be asymptomatic, their treatment can become challenging if wisdom teeth aren't removed during adolescence. Though uncommon, the delayed removal of wisdom teeth can be associated with the development of tumors.

Crowding

The presence of impacted wisdom teeth can play a role in the crowding of your teeth, especially noticeable in the front teeth, particularly the lower front teeth. This phenomenon is commonly observed after a patient has undergone braces treatment. Several factors contribute to teeth crowding following braces or in early adulthood, and retained, impacted wisdom teeth may be one of them. Even if there isn't an active problem at the time of consultation with the oral surgeon, the primary reason for removal is to proactively prevent potential long-term damage to your teeth, gums, and jaw bone.

Oral Assessment for Wisdom Teeth Removal

Through a comprehensive oral examination and X-rays of the mouth, our proficient dentists can assess the positioning of the wisdom teeth. This evaluation helps determine whether the teeth have erupted properly or may pose future issues. Research indicates that early assessment and intervention lead to better outcomes for patients. Typically, individuals are first assessed in their mid-teen years, either by their dentist, orthodontist, or an oral and maxillofacial surgeon. Early identification and management of potential concerns contribute to an improved overall prognosis.

Wisdom Teeth Extraction

Following the removal of the teeth, the gum is sutured, and a gauze is placed in the mouth for the patient to bite down on, aiding in controlling bleeding. Patients will be monitored under supervision in the office until they are ready to be taken home. Upon discharge, the post-operative kit will comprise instructions for the post-operative period, any required medication prescriptions, and details for a follow-up appointment if necessary.
